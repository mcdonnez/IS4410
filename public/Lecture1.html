Binary is a numbers systems in the powers of 2.

Lecture: History of Information Systems

The first person to come up with a computer was Charles Babbage, the father of the computer. He created blueprints for a machine to do calculations for this astronomy.

Moore's Law

The number of components we can place on a circuit will double every 18 months.
-Moore mid 1960s

What most people interpret this phrase to mean is that the speed of processing will double every 18 months. Some have thought of this as a self fulfilling processing. This is because companies usually used Moore's law to plan their future development.

Alan Turing

I propose to consider the question "Can machines think?"
-Turing 1950

"...once the machine thining method ahs started, it would not take long to outstrip our feeble powers. ... At some stae therefore we should expect the machines to take control...
-Turing 1951

Many of Turing's insights have fueled the discussion about the future of computers.

Charles Babbage, "The Father of the Computer"

Babbage Difference Engine Serial #2: This machine was never built in his lifetime. It was built by a musuem in the 1990s when blueprints of his machine were discovered. It was seven stacked up adding machines stacked on top of each other.

The Atanasoff-Berry Computer

First computer to use electricity as the energy source. Not many details are known how this machine worked. It's not very well known.

Harvard Mark I

Made all of the calculations necessary for atomic bombs. This was made for the Manhattan Project. It was great at calculating the blast radius for atomic bombs

Colossus

Accepted paper with holes in it to figure out how to crack the code. This is not the turin machine.

ENIAC

Developed by the United States at the end of WWII. It had a map of lines to set up a program. It took so long to setup a program that they would try to use that one program as much as possible. The output was a set of flashing lights.

1950s Era: UNIVAC

UNIVAC is the Universal Automatic Computer. First made in 1951 and successfully predicted the presidential prediction. Has many magnetic tapes to input information into the machine. There was still no kind of display or terminal that we are used to.

PDP-1

Has the first game ever called "Spaceware!"

Nolan Bushnell is an alumni of University of Utah and is one of the fathers of Video Games. He developed his own version of Spacewar and then invented the original Pong machine (1970s). This is where he made all of his money.

Atari 2600: 1978

Nolan Bushnell also created the first game station called Atari. These old electronics are very nice quality because they weren't made over seas.

IBM System/360

This machine could be customized so that you didn't need to buy everything. This put IBM on the map.

Supercomputers

CDC 600 (1965) and the Cray-1 (1976)
The CDC was centered in a circle to make the wiring shorter and make the cooling system smaller and more efficient. The Cray-1 cost $1.5 Million dollars and had the tenth of the computing power of an Iphone6.

Rise of the MiniComputer

  • DEC PDP-8

These computers fit on half of a desk instead of an entire room. The Data General Nova allowed the first MRI to run. The DEC PDP was one of the first computers on the internet.
